# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: non-technical SMB owners and founders of growing businesses (Toronto / North America). They arrive curious but cautious — they know AI automation could help but don't speak code, have been burned by hype, and need to trust that TRIVOX builds real systems, not vaporware. Their job-to-be-done: quickly decide whether TRIVOX is a credible, capable partner worth booking a call with. Context of use: evaluating on desktop or mobile, often comparing against generic agencies and SaaS tools.

## Product Purpose

trivox.ca is the marketing site for TRIVOX AI, a Toronto AI-automation agency that builds custom systems for growing businesses: 24/7 AI voice agents, chatbots, workflow automation, and intelligent CRM. The site exists to convert cautious SMB owners into booked discovery calls by demonstrating engineer-grade capability and earning trust fast. Success = a visitor who lands skeptical leaves convinced TRIVOX is the serious, technical partner (not another template shop) and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, technical, elite. Voice is confident and precise — the senior engineer who explains exactly what they'll build and why it works, without arrogance or buzzwords. Should evoke trust through competence: "these people clearly know systems." Dark-tech visual identity (deep navy-black, cyan signal-glow) signals technical seriousness. Tone stays grounded and direct, never hyped.

## Anti-references

- **Crypto / hype-bro**: no neon overload, fake urgency, "revolutionary/game-changing" buzzword soup, countdown-timer manipulation.
- **Cheap freelancer / Fiverr**: no clutter, mismatched fonts, low-trust stock-everything look.
- **Generic SaaS template**: no gradient blobs, no hero-metric stat template, no identical icon-heading-text card grids, no stock "glowing AI brain" imagery.

## Design Principles

- **Technical confidence, zero jargon.** Show engineer-grade depth through specificity and craft, but every word must land for a non-technical owner. Power without a glossary.
- **Earn trust before asking.** Concrete proof (named capabilities, real outcomes, working demos) precedes any CTA. The skeptic is the design target.
- **Show, don't tell.** Demonstrate the systems (interactive demos, real screens) rather than claiming them. Practice what we preach — the site itself is proof of capability.
- **Precision over flash.** Restraint reads as competence. Motion and the cyan glow are signals, not decoration; every effect earns its place.
- **Respect the visitor's intelligence and time.** Direct hierarchy, no manipulation, fast path from "what is this" to "book a call."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Body text must hit WCAG AA contrast (≥4.5:1) — critical on the dark `#030810` background where muted grays (`#A8B2C1`) risk washing out against tinted surfaces; verify and bump toward `#E8EAED` where close. Cyan `#00F0FF` is a signal accent, never the sole carrier of meaning (pair with text/shape). Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for all glow/scroll motion. Fully responsive and legible down to small mobile.
